Garadu River Area

Wildlife You Can See on Garadu River Area

Indian Flying Fox (Giant Fruit Bat)

  • One of the largest bats in the world, with wingspans up to 1.5–1.7 meters.
  • Best time to see: around sunset (5.30 PM – 6.30 PM) when they start flying.
  • Feed on: fruits, nectar, and flowers — mainly from mango, banana, and fig trees.

You can see hundreds flying together over Garadu River at dusk — a magical view!

Water Monitors (Kabaragoya)

  • Large semi-aquatic lizards, up to 1.5 meters long.
  • Excellent swimmers — often seen gliding quietly in the river.
  • Temperament: shy; they’ll swim away if you come too close.

Look near sunny riverbank spots in the morning — they like to warm up.

Kingfishers

  • Brightly colored birds with blue, orange, and white feathers.
  • perched on river branches or bamboo poles near the water.
  • dive sharply into water to catch small fish.

stay quiet and move slowly — they spook easily.

Small Fish & Crabs

  • Tiny silver fish dart below your kayak in clear water.
  • Mangrove crabs scuttle along roots and rocks at low tide.
  • hide when paddles splash, then return once water settles.

perfect area for nature photos and slow paddling to watch micro-life.

Tree Snakes (Non-venomous)

  • Rarely seen but beautiful green snakes moving through mangrove branches.
  • Common Green Whip Snake or Vine Snake.
  • motionless while hunting (small frogs and lizards) — blends perfectly with leaves.

look carefully among mangrove branches — a fun find for nature lovers.

Parrots and Other Tropical Birds

  • Common species: Rose-ringed Parakeet, Mynah, Bulbul, and Crows.
  • Feed on: fruits, seeds, and insects around mangroves.
  • early morning or late afternoon when they return to nests.

Bring binoculars for colorful parrot sightings across the river canopy.

Have Questions About Our Kayak Tours?

Do I need prior kayaking experience?

Not at all! Our tours are perfect for beginners. You’ll receive a full safety briefing and paddling instructions before your adventure begins. Our experienced guides will be with you at all times.

What should I bring for the tour?

We recommend bringing sunscreen, sunglasses, a hat, swimwear, and a towel. We’ll provide all kayaking gear, life jackets, and waterproof bags for your belongings.

Are life jackets provided?

Yes, life jackets are provided for every guest, and wearing them during the entire tour is mandatory for safety. We have sizes suitable for both adults and children.

What happens if the weather is bad?

Your safety comes first. If sea conditions are unsafe due to rain or strong winds, we’ll reschedule your tour for another convenient time or offer a full refund.
